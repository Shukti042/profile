Research

BanglaParaphrase: A synthtic Bangla Paraphrase dataset ensuring rich Diversity and Semantics

CO-Authors: Md. Ajwad Akil, Abhik Bhattacharya, Dr. Rifat Shahriyar

This research project was part of my undergraduate thesis where me and my thesis partner Md. Ajwad Akil worked under Dr. Rifat Shahriyar and attempted to solve two important problems of Bangla Natural Language Processing namely: Paraphrasing and Readable Text Simplification. We successfully curated a synthetic paraphrase dataset ensuring both diversity and semantics by designing a novel dataset filtering pipeline, two of the most important criteria for a high-quality paraphrase, and developed a transformer-based paraphrase generating model and presented rigorous analysis with existing Bangla Paraphrase datasets.

Status: This work is accepted in AACL

Unsupervised Bangla Text Simplification

CO-Authors: Md. Ajwad Akil, Dr. Rifat Shahriyar

This research project was also a part of my undergraduate thesis where we worked on Bangla Text Simplification without the need of a parallel corpus. We framed the problem of text Simplification as a style transfer problem and leveraging the parphrase generating model we developed as part of our thesis, we are attempting to simplify complex Bangla texts. As of now, The results look promising and we have identified the potential bottlenecks of the pipeline. We hope to ameliorate the performance upon further experimentations.

Status: Ongoing Research Project
